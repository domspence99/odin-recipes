Sushi

Description

A Philadelphia sushi roll is an inside-out roll featuring creamy, smoky, and fresh flavors. Core ingredients include salmon (smoked or raw sashimi-grade), cream cheese, nori (seaweed), sushi rice, cucumber, and toasted sesame seeds. Key steps are spreading rice on nori, flipping, filling with fish/cheese/cucumber, rolling tightly, topping with salmon, and slicing.

Ingredients (2 rolls)

  • Rice: 1 cup cooked sushi rice, seasoned with 2 tbsp rice vinegar, sugar, and salt.
  • Seaweed: 2 sheets of nori.
  • Fish: 4-6 oz smoked salmon or raw sashimi-grade salmon.
  • Cream Cheese: 2-3 oz cream cheese, cut into strips.
  • Vegetables: 1 small cucumber (julienned) and optional avocado.
  • Topping: Toasted sesame seeds.

Steps

  1. Prepare Noori & Rice: Place a half-sheet of nori (rough side up), on a bamboo mat. Spread a thin layer of seasoned sushi rice over the nori, leaving 1 cm of space at the top. Sprinkle with sesame seeds.
  2. Flip: Turn the sheet over so the rice is on the bottom against the plastic-wrapped mat.
  3. Fill: Place cream cheese strips, salmon, and cucumber in the center of the nori.
  4. Roll: Using the bamboo mat, lift the edge and roll tightly, creating a secure, round shape.
  5. Top & Slice: Add thin slices of salmon on top of the roll. Use a sharp, wet knife, to cut the roll into 8-10 pieces.
  6. Serve: Serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.
